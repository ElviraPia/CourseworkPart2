Benefits of the outdoors...

Benefit 1: Stress Reduction

The outdoor environment provides an escape from our daily routines,whether that be work, school or personal life stress- it has been proven by Harvard Medical School that spending as little as 20 minutes in an outdoor environment has reduced cortisol levels - a stress hormone - significantly. Read more on the article here!

Benefit 2: Improved Mood

Outdoor surroundings provide the tranquility and peace from our minds, lifting our spirits whilst simultaneously reducing any feelings of anxiety or depression. Especially with the company of family, friends or our beloved furry friends! - an article by HealthLine provides some more insight here!

Benefit 3: Social Interaction

Being in the outdoors is the perfect opportunity to get together with family and friends. Rain or shine, social interactions can impact mental wellbeing - grab your wellies, call a friend and spent time breathing in nature's fresh air.

Benefit 4: Physical Activity

Moving our bodies in the outdoor environment is beneficial,take care of your mental health by encouraging those around you to play a sport outside and you will feel a sense of relief, from whatever may be weighing you down. An NHS Article dives deeper into this, if you'd like to read more here!

Tips when in the outdoors...

Tip No.1: It is important to stay hydrated when in the outdoors, preventing dehydration especially in the summer months!

Tip No.2: Ensure you have comfortable clothing and footwear, as certain terrain may be dangerous without the correct equipment. As well as avoiding blisters and helping stability.

Tip No.3: Although weather in Scotland isn't always the most tropical! Try to check the weather forecast beforehand, to ensure an enjoyable time outdoors and avoid dangerous treks with high wind warnings and more.

Tip No.4: Ensure you know your chosen trail, avoiding any paths you may not be sure of - as much as being in the great outdoors is fun, safety must always come first.

Tip No.5: Always let someone know where you may are off adventuring or place emergency contacts in your phone, it's important to take precautions especially in remote outdoor areas.

Tip No.6: However, it is important to remember that nature is to be nurtured - we must treat all outdoor spaces with respect and not litter, after all we have a whole community to encourage and go IntoTheOutdoors!
